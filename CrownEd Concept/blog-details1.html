    How Can my Child Succeed at a Young Age?

    We live in the age of digitalization, with a generation that has grown up entirely on computers and phones. However, for prior generations and even today’s parents, it can be quite difficult to understand what this world has become and how it will continue to progress. In this age, how can parents ensure that their children are prepared for the future and set them up for success from a young age?

    In today's digital world, we often hear about young geniuses who have achieved remarkable academic feats such as graduating college as teenagers or achieving high scores on IQ tests. This leads to the question of how parents should prepare their children for early success in this age. Let's delve deeper into the lives of these young geniuses and examine the factors that contribute to their exceptional abilities.

    When discussing modern-day successes, we cannot overlook the impact of technology. The technological advancements of this era are so significant that even young children are capable of educating themselves. Many successful children have utilized coding as a tool in their journeys to becoming who they are today. For example, let's consider Tanmay Bakshi.

    • Author of "Hello Swift!"
    • Youngest IBM Watson Programmer
    • TEDx Speaker
    • Developer of Cognitive story
    • YouTube Educator

    Tanmay Bakshi is a Canadian resident of Indian origin, born in 2005 in Brampton, Canada. He developed a strong interest in technology from a young age and began coding at just 5 years old. By the age of 9, Tanmay had already created a YouTube channel to simplify complex programming concepts for beginners and fellow developers. However, as a young programmer, he often faced underestimation of his abilities and his skills were frequently overlooked in the adult-dominated industry.

    Despite the challenges, Tanmay was undeterred and eventually had a breakthrough with his project. He developed an AI-powered natural language processing system inspired by IBM's "Watson". This achievement brought Tanmay the recognition he needed, and at just 12 years old, he became IBM's youngest Watson programmer. Furthermore, it led to invitations to speak at prestigious conferences and events worldwide. Tanmay was no longer just a YouTube sensation; he had become a respected voice in the tech community.

    Beyond his technical achievements, Tanmay is driven by a mission to empower others. He believes that education is a fundamental right and that technology can bridge the gap between opportunity and accessibility. Tanmay has authored books, developed educational content, and conducted workshops to inspire and teach the next generation of innovators. He advocates for using AI and technology for social good, addressing issues like healthcare, education, and environmental sustainability.

    Tanmay Bakshi's story is a beacon of inspiration for young minds everywhere. His relentless pursuit of knowledge, coupled with his passion for teaching and helping others, demonstrates that age is no barrier to making a significant impact. Tanmay reminds us that with curiosity, dedication, and a willingness to share our gifts, we can all contribute to a brighter, more innovative future.

    Are you inspired by Tanmay’s story? Imagine if your child could be the next Tanmay Bakshi? We at Planet Success can help your young child learn the necessary skills to succeed in their career like Tanmay did. We, as your child’s mentors can help them gain the much needed knowledge and connections that they deserve as young coders. We can help teach your child the skills that they would require to be able to network and earn from an early age onwards.
